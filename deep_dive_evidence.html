Evidence & Prompts

What You Submit & How to Reflect

The portfolio is not a collection of completed tasks — it is evidence of class impact. Here's exactly what each badge submission requires and how your appraiser will evaluate your work.

What You Submit

Evidence Required for Each Badge

Every badge — regardless of focus area or strategy — requires the same three things. What changes is the nature of the artifact.

Every Badge Requires

The same evidence structure applies across all focus areas and all strategies

A reflection responding to four prompts — anchored in Future Ready impact and honest about what changed
An artifact showing visible evidence of student or instructional impact (student work, lesson design, data summary, etc.)
Evidence of what changed for students as a result of this work

Technology Requirement

Applies to the full portfolio — not every individual badge

At least one badge in your portfolio must use a technology-forward strategy from your focus area's menu. Technology may support your planning, your instruction, or your students' work — but it must show up meaningfully at least once.
🎯

Each submission should show not just what you did, but what changed for your students as a result. That's the heart of every badge.

Go Deeper

Reflection Prompts

Your reflection is where the real thinking happens. Answer these four prompts honestly for every badge — they connect your practice directly to the Future Ready framework and to your students' experience.

Four Prompts · Every Badge · Every Focus Area

  • 1

    What did you implement — and what were you hoping it would do for your students?

  • 2

    How did this move your classroom toward being Future Ready? What did students think, do, or experience that connects to that vision?

  • 3

    Reflecting honestly — how did this change what was possible in your classroom? What evidence do you have of impact on student learning?

  • 4

    What would you do differently next time — and where do you go next with this practice?

Appraiser Evaluation

How Your Portfolio Is Evaluated

Teachers earn a badge by submitting complete evidence. Appraisers evaluate the full portfolio holistically at year-end using a three-level rubric — no expertise in specific strategies required.
